Successful data platforms usually build three types of moats.
1. Network Contribution Moat
This is the most powerful moat.
Many data platforms grow their dataset through user activity.
Example model:
Users install a plugin or connect their email system.
The system analyzes email signatures or contact data (with consent).
Example signature:
Sarah Kim
VP Operations
NorthBridge Logistics
sarah.kim@northbridge.com
(214) 555-8392
That automatically enriches the platform database.
Historically, the company that became part of ZoomInfo built a huge dataset this way before the acquisition.
The more companies using the platform → the more data is contributed.
This creates network-driven data growth.
2. Proprietary Data Graph
The next moat is building a relationship graph instead of simple lists.
Basic contact databases store:
name
email
company
A more advanced system stores relationships like:
Company
   ↓
Departments
   ↓
Roles
   ↓
Employees
   ↓
Technologies used
Example insight:
Company uses Salesforce
VP of Sales hired 3 months ago
Hiring SDRs
That suggests a high probability of buying sales tools.
Graph data becomes extremely difficult for competitors to rebuild.
3. Continuous Verification Engine
Most contact databases decay rapidly.
Every year:
~30% of people change jobs
emails stop working
companies close or move
Platforms that dominate the market build continuous verification systems.
Verification methods include:
SMTP email testing
domain monitoring
job change detection
website rescanning
social signals
Every record receives something like:
confidence score
verification timestamp
data source reliability
This creates higher accuracy than competitors.
Accuracy is one of the main reasons companies stay with a platform.
What the Best Systems Combine
The strongest sales intelligence platforms combine:
large data sources
+
network contributions
+
relationship graphs
+
continuous verification
That combination creates a durable data advantage.
Why This Matters for a New Platform
The first version of your system might start with:
crawled company websites
business registries
directories
But the real long-term value comes when the system starts learning from:
user activity
CRM integrations
email patterns
job changes
buying signals
That transforms the platform from a static database into a living intelligence network.
✅ In simple terms:
Old sales databases = lists of contacts
Modern AI systems = dynamic company intelligence graphs
