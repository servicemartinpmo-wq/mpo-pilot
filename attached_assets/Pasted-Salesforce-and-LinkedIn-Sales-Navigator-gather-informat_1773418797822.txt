Salesforce and LinkedIn Sales Navigator gather information from different sources, but the underlying principle is the same: they aggregate data from public sources, user-contributed content, and integrations. Here’s a breakdown of each:
1. Salesforce
Salesforce gets its information from multiple channels, depending on which features or modules you’re using (e.g., CRM, Sales Cloud, Data.com, or third-party integrations):
Manual Data Entry: Users input data directly, such as contacts, leads, accounts, opportunities.
Integrations & APIs: Salesforce can pull data from email platforms (Gmail, Outlook), marketing automation tools, and ERP systems.
Third-Party Data Providers: Salesforce used to own Data.com, which aggregated business information from public filings, government registries, and crowd-sourced updates. Even after its retirement, similar functionality exists via third-party apps on the Salesforce AppExchange.
Web Scraping & Public Records: Some Salesforce partners collect information from publicly available sources such as company websites, press releases, LinkedIn profiles, SEC filings, and industry databases.
User Contributions / Enrichment Tools: Some Salesforce features allow users to enrich lead data automatically using AI or integrated services like ZoomInfo, Clearbit, or InsideView.
2. LinkedIn Sales Navigator
LinkedIn Sales Navigator is specialized in professional and social data:
LinkedIn Profiles: The primary source is LinkedIn users’ publicly shared profiles — work experience, education, skills, endorsements, and public posts.
Behavioral Data on LinkedIn: Actions like job changes, shares, connections, or updates are captured to infer lead activity and engagement.
Company Pages & News: LinkedIn aggregates information from company pages, press releases, and official announcements to keep company insights current.
User-Contributed Data: When users update their own profiles or engage with content, that signals accurate data points for the system.
Third-Party Integrations: Sales Navigator integrates with CRMs (including Salesforce), enabling additional enrichment from those databases.
Key Difference Between the Two
Salesforce: More enterprise-focused; gathers data from internal business systems, third-party enrichment services, and direct user input.
Sales Navigator: More social-focused; gathers data mainly from LinkedIn user activity, public company information, and professional networking signals.
