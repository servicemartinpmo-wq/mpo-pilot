Stage 1 — Structured Business Registries (Fastest Foundation)
Start with sources that already contain structured company records.
Examples in the U.S.:
state Secretary of State business registries
city business license directories
nonprofit registries
federal contractor databases
These often include:
company name
address
officers or directors
filing dates
industry classification
If you aggregate all U.S. state registries, you can reach tens of millions of registered businesses quickly.
The crawler pipeline looks like:
State registry
   ↓
bulk data extraction
   ↓
normalized company records
   ↓
master company database
This step creates the base company list.
Stage 2 — Industry Directories & Associations
Next layer: structured industry directories.
Examples:
chambers of commerce
trade associations
professional guilds
vendor marketplaces
These often include:
company websites
executive names
phone numbers
industries
employee counts
Because they are curated, the data quality is higher than random web scraping.
Your earlier work verifying chamber listings is actually exactly the kind of dataset these platforms collect.
Stage 3 — Web Crawling for Company Websites
Once you have company domains, crawlers can scan those sites.
The system extracts:
leadership pages
contact pages
press releases
job postings
Example:
company.com/about
company.com/team
company.com/contact
company.com/news
AI entity extraction identifies:
CEO
VP of Sales
CTO
HR Director
This expands each company record with people and roles.
Stage 4 — Contact Intelligence
This stage creates the sales lead layer.
Steps:
detect email pattern for the domain
predict employee emails
verify deliverability
assign confidence score
Example:
Company: northbridge.com
Employee: Sarah Kim
Pattern: first.last@northbridge.com
Predicted email: sarah.kim@northbridge.com
SMTP verification checks if it’s valid.
This step converts company data into usable sales leads.
Stage 5 — Signal Detection
Once your database is large, you add buying signals.
The system monitors:
job listings
funding announcements
hiring spikes
new product launches
leadership hires
Example:
Company hiring 5 cybersecurity engineers
→ strong security software buying signal
Signals allow your platform to prioritize opportunities automatically.
Resulting Database Structure
Your final dataset becomes a company intelligence graph.
Core objects:
Companies
Contacts
Roles

Domains
Email patterns
Technologies
Signals
Locations
Relationships allow queries like:
Find VP-level leaders at logistics companies
with 50-200 employees
in Texas
hiring operations managers
Why This Strategy Works
Random web crawling alone is inefficient.
Starting with structured sources provides:
verified company names
real domains
geographic data
industry classification
This creates a high-quality starting graph that crawlers can expand.
