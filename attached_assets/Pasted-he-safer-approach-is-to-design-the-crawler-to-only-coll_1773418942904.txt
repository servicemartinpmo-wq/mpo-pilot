Below is the legal architecture most data companies use.
1. The “Legal Web Crawling” Model
Many lead-data companies gather information by combining three sources.
A. Public Web Crawling
Your AI crawler can scan public pages that allow bots.
Examples:
Company websites (team pages, contact pages)
Blog posts and press releases
Public directories
Chamber of commerce listings
Government registries
News sites
The crawler checks:
robots.txt
page metadata
rate limits
If the site allows crawling, the AI extracts structured data like:
company name
executives
email patterns
phone numbers
address
industry
hiring signals
B. Public Government & Institutional Databases
These are very powerful and completely legal sources.
Examples in the U.S.:
U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission filings
State business registries
City business licenses
Federal contractor databases
Nonprofit registries
These often contain:
executives
addresses
phone numbers
ownership records
C. Official APIs
Instead of scraping platforms directly, companies use APIs.
Examples:
LinkedIn (limited official APIs)
Clearbit
ZoomInfo
Hunter.io
Apollo.io
Your app could call these APIs to enrich leads.
2. How Data Companies Actually Build This
A typical architecture looks like this:
Crawler Layer
  ↓
Data Extraction AI
  ↓
Normalization Engine
  ↓
Lead Enrichment
  ↓
Verification Engine
  ↓
CRM Database
  ↓
Sales Dashboard
AI handles:
deduplicating companies
matching people to companies
validating emails
detecting role titles
detecting industries
3. The Smart Trick Most Lead Platforms Use
Instead of scraping restricted sites directly, they:
Crawl company websites
Crawl press releases
Crawl job postings
Crawl public directories
Infer employee data from patterns
Example:
CEO: John Smith
Company: ABC Logistics
Email pattern: jsmith@abclogistics.com
The AI predicts and verifies the email.
4. A Feature That Would Help Your App
Since you’re already verifying chamber-of-commerce companies, you could build a crawler that automatically:
Finds a business name
Locates the official website
Extracts leadership
Finds email patterns
Verifies phone numbers
Adds it to your CRM
This would automate exactly what you're currently doing manually.
5. What NOT to Do (Legal Risk)
Avoid building systems that:
scrape logged-in platforms
create fake accounts to harvest data
bypass CAPTCHA or security protections
scrape personal data from social platforms without permission
Courts have allowed scraping of public web data, but bypassing security protections can cause legal issues.
